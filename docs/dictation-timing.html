How to read it: each lane in A/B is a UML-timing-diagram lifeline — high means active. In trace B the yellow prefill lane fires in short bursts throughout the dictation (each burst seats one word-boundary chunk of the transcript into the kv cache) and finishes with the 0.55 s tail; in trace A the same ~5.4 s of GPU work stands as one solid block after the speech ends. Strip C re-zeroes the clock at the last spoken word: yellow is the silence until the first token, violet is decode until the first speakable sentence.

Why E4B's gap is shorter than its decode rate suggests: on this prompt the E4B answers with no thought channel — its violet segment is the 19-word sentence alone — while the 12B spends most of its violet on thought tokens before the sentence begins. Template-induced thought length remains the largest single TTFS variable.

Method: the measured runs deliver the transcript at 30 words/s ('T' frames of 5 words, ttft_dictate.py, client-side clocks); at a natural dictation pace (~2.5 words/s) the hiding is more comfortable still. Decode runs the production configuration — MTP block-3 speculative decoding; MTP does not move first-token time, only the sentence. The transcript lane's commit cadence is drawn at voicecat's real-whisper rhythm (a ~0.6 s pass every 2.5 s). Burst delivery — the whole text at once with nothing to hide behind — should use a plain line instead, which is the unchanged path. Prefill ids are 930 with byte-identical replies in every delivery mode, both models: word-boundary splits reproduce the exact token stream.
